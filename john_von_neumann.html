Ludwig Bieberbach

Ludwig Bieberbach was a mathematician and eventually a sympathizer with the Nazi regime in his later years. He was born on December 4, 1886, in Riedstadt, Germany, and died in Oberaudorf, Germany, on September 1, 1982. He did not later apologize or face any regret for supporting the Nazi regime, nor did he publicly or privately condemn it after WWII when Nazi Germany fell.

Ludwig Bieberbach birth place circa 1880s around ridestadt and the hessian region

Mathematical contributions

He had famous mathematical contributions during his most active years from the 1920s to late 1930s. One of his major innovations was the Bieberbach Conjecture. He also founded the Nazi math league Deutsche Mathematik, which offered a different perspective of math influenced by Nazi racial studies and opinions. Additionally, he solved Hilbert's 18th problem. After the war, during the denazification process, he was removed from all academic and educational institutions.
