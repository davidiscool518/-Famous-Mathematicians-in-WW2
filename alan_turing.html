Alan Turing (1912–1954)

Alan Turing was a mathematician born on June 23, 1912, in Maida Vale, London. He died on June 7, 1954, in Wilmslow, United Kingdom. He was a mathematician, codebreaker, and one of the founders of early digital computers and modern computing as we know it today.

Circa 1912 — Maida Vale, London, Alan Turing's birthplace

Alan Turing Major innovations and the begining of the modern digtal computer systems of today

He was one of the early pioneers of computing with his first concept: the Turing machine, named after himself. This computer was a mathematical model for a universal computing device which could simulate any algorithm, an innovation that allows for modern computers. He also made innovations that would later help aid the war effort in World War II. In 1939, he joined Bletchley Park, which was the secret code-breaking headquarters. This was the first time a military had tried to disable technology in this way, leading to the beginning of cyber warfare. He also helped develop the Bombe, which was one of the major initiatives that helped the Allies decrypt a massive 43,000 messages per month..
